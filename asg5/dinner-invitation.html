Join me for an unforgettable evening with three remarkable guests. The Menu will be filled with delicious cuisine from my culture!

Frida Kahlo

Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by the nature and artifacts of Mexico. Her work was influenced by indigenous Mexican culture and European artistic traditions.

I would love to have Frida over because her resilience in the face of physical and emotional pain is incredibly inspiring. I'm fascinated by her unique perspective on life, art, and identity, and I'd love to discuss how she transformed suffering into beautiful, meaningful art.

Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who was active as a painter, draughtsman, engineer, scientist, theorist, sculptor, and architect.

Having Leonardo at my dinner table would be a dream come true. His unparalleled curiosity and ability to excel in both arts and sciences is something I deeply admire. I'd love to ask him about his creative process and how he managed to bridge the worlds of art and science so seamlessly.

Michelle Obama

Michelle Obama is an American attorney and author who served as the first lady of the United States from 2009 to 2017. She is a graduate of Princeton University and Harvard Law School.

I admire Michelle Obama for her grace, intelligence, and commitment to important social causes. Her initiatives on education, health, and poverty have made a real difference. I'd love to hear her perspectives on leadership, public service, and balancing family life with public responsibilities.
